## Scope — five workstreams (roadmap-enriched)

1. **WS1 Sub-part detail passes** — 1–2px identity accents, ONE dominant per creature (slit/round eyes, horns, claws, scale-dots), palette-locked, drawn off part bounding boxes.
2. **WS2 Rim / back-light pass — DEFERRED (mid-execution, user-approved) to a NEW view-layer shader Part.** A baked finalize post-pass conflicts irreconcilably with the sel-out/dissolve invariants: modifying *finalized* interior pixels either strands orphans (rim after dissolve) or perturbs the outline — ink-2×2 tips, coverage loss (rim before dissolve), unstable to every filter/ordering across the roster's varied geometries (6 attempts). A back-light rim is a view-layer material/shader effect (reuses the shipped raw-Godot-node VFX infra, per the Signature VFX Layer), NOT a palette-bake pass. Carved to a new Part **"Unit Back-Light Shader (view-layer)"**. WS1/3/4/5 unaffected — they are draw-time geometry, not post-passes.
3. **WS3 Squash-and-stretch + lagged secondary motion** — tail/wings lag main motion 1–2 frames; squash on idle/walk/attack. ENHANCEMENT layered on the keyframe system, not a rewrite. Must not break `ground_y`/`airborne` lint.
4. **WS4 Big-unit internal volume** *(roadmap PRIORITY)* — at ≥48px the single-ramp torso reads as a flat slab; add internal torso volume + thick shaded limb mass for large(48)/colossal(64)/dragon(80–96) tiers.
5. **WS5 Form-aware banding lint** — rim-light + internal shading add bands the canvas-scaled `band_limit` was loosened to tolerate "for now" (`lint.py:306-310` handoff comment names THIS Part); make banding form/part-aware via exempt channels and tighten the loosened tolerance.

**Out of scope:** `quarry_slinger` T-pose (shipped in Part B, `props.py:173`); silhouette differentiation (Part B — §11 Principle 1: detail is additive polish, NOT a substitute for silhouette distinctness); backgrounds/audio/UI/3D (design Non-Goals); re-adding a redundant *global* shading pass (the pipeline already has single-light directional shading + AO — `apply_directional_shading` skeletons.py:231).
